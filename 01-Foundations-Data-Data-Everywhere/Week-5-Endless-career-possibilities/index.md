## Business Problems and Data Analytics

Data analytics helps businesses make better decisions. But it doesn’t start with data. It starts with a business need.

The process usually looks like this:
1. Identify a business problem.
2. Find relevant data related to that problem.
3. Analyze the data.
4. Use the results to make decisions.

Sometimes analysis answers the original question. Other times, it leads to new questions that need further investigation.

Most businesses use data analytics to deal with:

- **Issues** – Topics that need investigation.
- **Questions** – Requests for specific information.
- **Problems** – Obstacles that need solutions.

These become the foundation of a **business task**.

A **business task** is the specific problem or question that data analysis is meant to solve.

Everything in data analytics begins with a clear business task.

## Fairness in Data Analysis

Data analysts have an important responsibility: ensuring fairness.

**Fairness** means making sure that analysis does not create or reinforce bias.

Fairness should be considered:
- When collecting data
- When analyzing data
- When presenting results

To maintain fairness, analysts should:
- Use inclusive and representative samples
- Consider social and environmental context
- Be cautious with self-reported data
- Avoid making unsupported conclusions

## Case Study: Voluntary Workshop

A high school offered a voluntary workshop for teachers. Out of 43 teachers, 19 chose to attend.

At the end of the year, students rated teachers from 1 (very poor) to 6 (very good).

Results:
- Teachers who attended: average score of 4.95  
- Teachers who did not attend: average score of 4.22  

The administration concluded that the workshop was successful.

### Why This Is Problematic

The workshop was voluntary, not randomly assigned.

This means:
- More motivated or higher-performing teachers may have chosen to attend.
- The higher scores may not have been caused by the workshop.
- Other explanations cannot be ruled out.

This is an example of potential bias.

### How to Improve the Study

To make it more reliable:
- Randomly select teachers to attend the workshop.
- Measure outcomes directly related to workshop techniques.
- Collect additional data to support conclusions.

Correlation does not automatically mean causation.

## Data Analyst Roles

The title “data analyst” is one of many roles that include the word “analyst.” Different roles focus on different responsibilities.

Some related roles include:

- **Business Analyst** – Improves processes, products, or services using data.
- **Data Analytics Consultant** – Evaluates and improves data systems.
- **Data Engineer** – Prepares and organizes data for analysis.
- **Data Scientist** – Uses advanced tools and models to identify trends.
- **Data Specialist** – Focuses on managing and organizing databases.
- **Operations Analyst** – Analyzes workflows and performance.

Although these roles sound similar, their responsibilities differ.

---

## Job Specializations in Data Analytics

Data analytics also includes industry-specific roles.

Examples include:

- **Marketing Analyst** – Studies market trends and product demand.
- **HR/Payroll Analyst** – Reviews payroll data and workforce efficiency.
- **Financial Analyst** – Evaluates financial data and company performance.
- **Risk Analyst** – Assesses financial and business risks.
- **Healthcare Analyst** – Analyzes medical and hospital data to improve operations.

---

## Key Takeaway

Data analytics begins with a business problem.

Good analysts:
- Ask the right questions
- Use fair and unbiased methods
- Interpret results carefully
- Understand that data must support conclusions

Clear thinking and fairness are just as important as technical skills.
