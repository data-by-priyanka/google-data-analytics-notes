## Analytical Skills

Analytical skills are the abilities that help us solve problems using facts and logical thinking.

### Important Aspects of Analytical Skills

**1. Curiosity**  
Curiosity means wanting to learn and understand more. A good analyst always asks questions and tries to dig deeper instead of accepting information at face value.

**2. Understanding Context**  
Context means understanding the situation or environment in which something happens. Data does not exist alone. It is always connected to a business, process, or real-world condition.

**3. Grouping Information**  
This means organizing data into categories so it becomes easier to understand. Grouping helps in seeing patterns and making comparisons.

**4. Technical Mindset**  
A technical mindset means breaking a big problem into smaller parts and solving it step by step in a logical way.

**5. Data Design**  
Data design is about organizing information in a structured way so it is easy to analyze and use.

**6. Data Strategy**  
Data strategy focuses on managing the people, tools, and processes used in data analysis. It ensures that data is handled properly and used effectively.

Data-driven decision-making combines curiosity, context understanding, technical thinking, data design, and data strategy.

## Analytical Thinking

Analytical thinking is the process of identifying a problem and solving it using data in a clear and structured way.

### Key Aspects of Analytical Thinking

**1. Visualization**  
Visualization means presenting data in charts, graphs, or dashboards. It helps people understand complex information quickly.

**2. Strategy**  
Strategy helps analysts define goals and decide how to use data to achieve them. It ensures that data collection and analysis are purposeful.

**3. Problem-Oriented Approach**  
This means keeping the main problem in focus throughout the project. Every step of the analysis should aim to solve that specific problem.

**4. Correlation**  
Correlation is the relationship between two variables.  
However, correlation does not mean causation. Just because two things move together does not mean one causes the other.

**5. Big-Picture and Detail-Oriented Thinking**  
Big-picture thinking helps you understand overall goals and direction.  
Detail-oriented thinking ensures accuracy and proper execution.  
A good analyst balances both.

The more flexible your thinking, the easier it becomes to generate new ideas and solutions.

Critical thinking helps you ask the right questions.  
Creative thinking helps you discover new and unexpected answers.

## Root Cause Analysis

A root cause is the main reason why a problem happens.  
If we identify and remove the root cause, we can prevent the problem from happening again.

### The Five Whys

The Five Whys technique involves asking “Why?” repeatedly, usually five times, to find the deeper cause of a problem.

By the fifth answer, you often uncover the actual root cause instead of just addressing surface-level issues.

---

## Gap Analysis

Gap analysis is a method used to compare the current situation with a desired future state.

It helps answer:
- Where are we now?
- Where do we want to be?
- What is stopping us from getting there?

Once the gaps are identified, we can plan steps to close them.

Gap analysis can improve efficiency, accessibility, performance, and overall results.

---

## Identifying Missing Elements

Sometimes the most important question is:  
“What did we not consider?”

Thinking this way helps identify missing steps, overlooked data, or weak areas in a process. This improves decision-making and strategy.

---

## Quartile

A quartile divides a dataset into four equal parts.

It helps us understand how data is distributed and where a specific value stands compared to the rest of the data.
